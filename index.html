Enes DURAN

University of Tübingen, Tübingen / Germany· enes.duran@uni-tuebingen.de

I am a PhD student working on Human-Object Interaction and its possible applications to Robotics. These span 3D Computer Vision, Virtual Humans, Human-Object Interaction, and Robust Reinforcement Learning.

I graduated with a Master's in Machine Learning at the University of Tübingen. At the same time, I was a Research Assistant at the Perceiving Systems Department at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems. I had the chance to work under the supervision of Dr. Muhammed Kocabas, Dr. Omid Taheri, Dr. Vasileios Choutas, and Prof. Michael Black. My focus has been on Human Pose and Shape Estimation and Hand-Object interaction.

During my bachelor years at Bilkent University, I studied sample-efficient Reinforcement Learning.
